This project brings history to life by capturing a day in the life of civil rights activists in NYC, highlighting the crucial yet often-overlooked role of spaces like small businesses in supporting Black resistance against both historical and present-day racism. By mapping the locations where activists organized, protested, and built community, we broaden the concept of preservation to include the lived experiences of these movements, recognizing everyday spaces as integral to the fight for justice. This approach reimagines what is considered historically significant, honoring the resilience embedded in these sites and inspiring a more inclusive understanding of our past and its ongoing influence on the present.About
